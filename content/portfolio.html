A selection of some of my work. View it as a struggle to resolve a passion for computing and the desire to impact the physical environment.

small red tile · 2010

Design of an appropriate cooking device for rural Nicaragua to replace traditional open fires. Result was an invention that burns rice husk like nothing else in the world.

MDG actors · 2009

A digital narrative that weaves stories of people doing good in the world with ways to find out more about their work. Built with natural language processing and mashed-up APIs.

ewb-gt.org · 2010

Engineers Without Borders was generous in helping my work with cook stoves, so I donated some design and code to their organization. The site balances information density and shows off their compelling pictures.

count char · 2010

(iPhone + Python) to automate the task of separating burned from unburned char while doing cook stove experiments at the Aprovecho Research Center.

ray trace · 2007

A ground-up implementation of ray tracing in MATLAB. The programming was easy but the physics were challenging: how does light bounce off of a surface? What about a translucent surface? Or a colored surface?

foo-lab · 2010

Some friends and I were curious about what our circle's global footprint would look like. I used the Wufoo form API to automatically turn survey results into a map of where our friends had been.

rough draft · 2010

Made by a team of engineers that lets out their creative frustration every year at Campus Movie Fest. We've been shown at the Georgia Tech finale three times. Rough Draft was our best work.

guilford's hell · 2008

Interactive fiction games provide a solid basis for meta-creative experiments. As a writer/programmer, you're looking to come up with an experience that is compelling and fun for users. That means crafting an interesting world with mind-engaging puzzles.
